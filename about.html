Hi, I'm Maria!

My name is Maria Galindo. I'm a 21-year-old University of Florida student born in Bogota, Colombia. When I was 10, I moved to Ft. Lauderdale, Florida, learned English and fell in love with writing.

I moved to Gainesville, Florida in 2010 with dreams of getting my Journalism degree at UF and joining the ranks of the guardians of the first amendment. This all, however, changed when I was forced to take microeconomics as a required elective, and I found my second love – efficiency. The concept of marginal analysis just made sense to me, so I began exploring the career paths that would allow me to put my intellectual curiosity of efficiency to work.

After graduation, I'll be moving to New York City to work in operations. And while I won't be writing, I will always be a writer. In fact, in my free time I write a career blog. I write about my thoughts on career and experiences in corporate america.

I’m an ENTJ and a Gryffindor. When I’m not writing or problem solving, I can likely be found reading, working on student government projects, traveling or thinking about traveling. I’m definitely always on the lookout for a new adventure. Although I’ve only been to three continents, it is my goal to make it to every single one before I turn 50.

I’ve also been known to dabble in photography and graphic design, mostly because I've been completely inspired to do so by some of my amazing professors at the Univerisity of Florida.
